About Me

I’m a cognitive scientist and philosopher researching how prosody influences sentence processing, mental representation, and the nature of linguistic interpretation. I combine methods from psycholinguistics and philosophy of mind to better understand the limits of reanalysis and the role of structure in language comprehension.

Research

  • Garden-Path Sentences & Prosody: Exploring how helpful prosodic boundaries aid syntactic reanalysis and reduce lingering misinterpretations.
  • Mental Representation & Realism: Critiquing metaphysical commitments in cognitive science through experimental and theoretical analysis.
  • Bayesian Parsing Models: Developing cue-based models that account for interaction between syntax, prosody, and semantics.
